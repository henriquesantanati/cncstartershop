## Key Findings vs the $500 Target

1. **$500 is realistic and proven.** Every niche has at least one site at or near the visit level
   that supports $500/mo: hobbylasercutters (11K laser), outdoortechlab (10K power),
   cncrouterinfo (7.4K CNC and climbing), mechanicinsider (30K detailing via ads).

2. **New sites CAN ramp fast in these underserved niches RIGHT NOW.** carmechan went 143->8,736
   in 3 months. cncrouterinfo went 1,380->7,416. outdoortechlab grew steadily. This is the single
   most encouraging finding: consistent publishing produces fast traffic growth here today.

3. **Thin content gets demoted.** mechanicinsider (52K->30K) and geekbitz (5.6K->1.7K) are both
   declining with 80%+ bounce rates. Fast growth from thin content does NOT last. The winning play
   is fast ramp (like carmechan) WITH quality that holds (like hobbylasercutters/cncrouterinfo,
   which have 38-43% bounce and 2.6-3.0 ppv).

4. **Daily publishing is the lever.** Competitors hit these traffic levels publishing sporadically.
   A daily, quality-gated, internally-linked pipeline can plausibly reach 10-12K visits faster than
   they did, because traffic in these niches scales with the number of ranking long-tail articles
   (each keyword is only 500-2,400/mo, so you need 30-60 ranking articles to aggregate to 10-12K).

5. **Per-niche revenue model differs:**
   - LASER / POWER / CNC = affiliate-led. Reach ~10-12K visits via 50-80 articles.
   - DETAILING = display-led. Needs ~25-30K visits; per-sale ($6) is too low for affiliate alone.
     Viable but the slowest path to $500 and dependent on ad RPM.
